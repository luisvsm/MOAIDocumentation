by ibisum » Tue Dec 04, 2012 8:24 am

Something has been bothering me about the MOAI scene lately, and I've just now worked out what it is: where the hell are Zipline?

Are you guys just under the radar/in crunch mode/ignoring us?

There have been many threads lately where I've thought "surely, someone from Zipline is going to comment on this" .. but nada.

Also, this lack of attention to 'the scene' from Zipline manifests itself in other ways. How many forks and patches and random branches of MOAI are out there - right now, it seems like *A LOT*. Zipline, please, can you please do something to address the fracturing situation thats going on: get a much smoother flow going on merging pull requests from the community. Lately it seems like a few people are branching because there is no way to get stuff merged into mainline (Linux support, Audio fixes, other bug fixes) and when it does occur, it happens in long intervals. I think, with all the effort the community is putting into moving MOAI forward, Zipline needs to be paying a lot more attention on a daily/weekly basis, rather than monthly, right now. We need to nurture this scene, guys ..

Ok, random question. To me, the biggest concern with Zipline's support for the open source side of MOAI is that we have to wait for these busy people to pull useful changes into the moai-dev github, because we don't own it. A repo that the community could be responsible for that we can collectively contribute to, and that would be maintained/controlled by more active and experienced MOAI developers that are part of the community here (and are willing to give up some of their time to do so)?
