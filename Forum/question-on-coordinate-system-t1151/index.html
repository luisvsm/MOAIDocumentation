Re: Question on coordinate system

by ibisum » Mon Aug 27, 2012 8:36 am

There is one very good reason to use 0,0 in cartesian mode with origin in the center of the screen: resolution independence. When you arrange your resources and game entities around the center of the screen, it becomes a lot easier to deal with different aspect ratios and resolutions on the X/Y axes.

It took me a little while to get used to the whole setup, but actually after a year I think this is really the best approach to the whole issue. 0,0 at top/left or bottom/left just doesn't make sense to me any more, in fact.

FWIW, I found it very helpful to have a .png around with the various coordinate systems, so I wrote a "Screen Grid Maker" tool, which you can find out about here: simple-grid-maker-tool-in-processing-t485

You can use it to generate a .png that you can assign to Props that will give you a bit of a hint in terms of how things are oriented - check it out.

Re: Question on coordinate system

by sgeos » Mon Aug 27, 2012 10:01 am

ibisum wrote:There is one very good reason to use 0,0 in cartesian mode with origin in the center of the screen: resolution independence.

I thought viewport:setScale() solves this problem. If you alway set the scale of one of your axes to the same value, it does not matter where the origin is located.

The size of the other axis does not matter either. Just scale it to the first one. If X is your reference axis, your viewport will have a variable height. If Y is your reference axis, your viewport will have a variable width.

Code: Select all
  1. -- Screen resolution (self is the state object)

  2. self.screenWidth  = MOAIEnvironment.horizontalResolution or 1024

  3. self.screenHeight = MOAIEnvironment.verticalResolution   or 9 / 16 * self.screenWidth

  4.  

  5. -- Viewport

  6. self.viewport = MOAIViewport.new ( )

  7. self.scaleX  = 2048 -- X reference axis

  8. self.scaleY  = self.scaleX * self.screenHeight / self.screenWidth

  9. self.viewport:setSize (  self.screenWidth, self.screenHeight )

  10. self.viewport:setScale ( self.scaleX,      self.scaleY       )


Translating the origin is just a couple of addition problems. FWIW, I find it easier to place UI elements with the origin in the center.
