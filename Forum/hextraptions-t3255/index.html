[WiP] Hextraptions

by veli » Sat Feb 27, 2016 4:15 pm

I'm working on hexagonal puzzle game based on digital logic and programmable toys. Currently only the core mechanics exist and I don't actually know what the game is about. I'm still experimenting with stuff.



You can place bunch of logical blocks on hexagonal grids and they will be evaluated in cycles. You can also place toys and watch them interact. There is special toy that is controlled by keyboard. This toy in the middle is always trying to orient itself towards the player:



Some building blocks and toys are scripted in Lua, but you can also create new empty toy and add custom logic in game. Toys can have components (sight, compass, radio, thruster...) to make sense of their surrounding and to move around.

The cool part is that you can enter any toy and get same hexagonal grid. Then you place inputs, outputs and logic blocks to create wiring, and the toy will behave as programmed.

This toy moves away from anything that comes near:

It is wired by simply connecting directional sight sensor to thruster that moves the toy in opposite direction:

You can also see what inputs and outputs are currently active. There is a shortcut to pause/resume game, so you can "debug" your creations.

There's still ton of work remaining on GUI, sounds & music, saving & loading, and making an actual game out of it. Still, the core part is progressing quite nicely thanks to awesome powers of Lua and Moai.
