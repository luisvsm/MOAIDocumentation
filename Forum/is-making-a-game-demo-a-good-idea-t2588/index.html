by naturally » Mon Mar 24, 2014 5:14 pm

This is my strategy as well, first N levels free via demo. It really depends on your game though. Some games are more amenable to ads, some games do better with upfront pay. I'd say a game like Dead Dark would benefit from this model too. You will just likely scare off a lot of people without a demo. Also I would not recommend releasing a demo until the game is polished and done, players will drop out after trying out a half-finished demo.

There are players, like myself, that are turned off by freemium ad-filled games that are constantly trying to sell me. I'd rather pay for a gem than get a free turd. I'm probably in a niche market tho...

It's tricky though because I'm not sure how to offer both the demo and the pay version side-by-side. I'd rather have just one storefront link with both choices available.

But... now I'm on the fence after watching the video...

Re: Is making a game demo a good idea?

by scottwb » Sat Jan 31, 2015 11:24 am

We have two game models:

- freemium with ads - unlock for $1
- paid app $1.99

It seems from our experience and the experience of other developers I know that the freemium ad model is now dead.

Couple of reasons:

- the advertising platforms are in a race to the bottom and therefore the payout for ads to the developer is dropping rapidly
- the big players are moving their advertising budgets to other platforms e.g. TV, Facebook, Twitter
- users are completely turned off by ads in games now, as in order to make revenue on ads, the developers are having to increase the frequency. It's a death spiral

I'm still considering the options and best way forward for 2015.

Re: Is making a game demo a good idea?

by Vavius » Sat Jan 31, 2015 1:02 pm

I can confirm from my experience that free versions of a paid apps in mobile market don't have positive effect on sales.
And having both versions (free and paid) at the same time can completely ruin paid downloads.

It's hard to make a good demo - it must be interesting enough to excite player, but keep in secret as many gameplay aspects as possible. While trying to find a best point to convince player to buy a full version developers came to f2p model that totaly dominates the market today. Anything else doesn't work.

Okay, highly advertised Indie titles pricing at $4-10 with kickstarter support or some user base before release can earn some money too...
