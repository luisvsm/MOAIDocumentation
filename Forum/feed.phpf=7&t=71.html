Hi all!  New to Moai here, absolutely love it so far.  Especially excited about better audio support.



So at this time does anyone know if UNTZ is suitable for developing music games that require very low latency and synchronized playback of multiple audio sound files?  I realize Android's audio capabilities are behind that of iOS, but how might a Moai/UNTZ solution compare in performance to something written directly in an iOS environment?




I think UNTZ would be a great solution for a music game.  UNTZ can have very low latency on iOS, OSX, and Windows, unfortunately high latency (approx. 100ms) is currently unavoidable on Android 2.2.  The output buffer size controls the latency and that is set when you initialize UNTZ (1024, 512, 256, etc.).  Currently, there is no way to exactly sync two or more sounds, but calling play consecutively on each sound object gets you pretty close.  We are rapidly developing UNTZ on multiple platforms, if there are features that you would like to see incorporated then please let us know, or grab the source and go to town!

The UNTZ library was designed to offer everything mobile developers need to easily add cross-platform audio management and playback support to their projects. As a central component of the Moai framework, UNTZ will be continually updated as new platforms & devices are added to the SDK. The Moai open-source license ensures that developers have the freedom & flexibility to use and extend the library as needed for their projects.

UNTZ was developed by Retronyms. Retronyms' background as a developer of high-performance music apps like Rebirth, FourTrack, StudioTrack and DopplerPad, and innovative mobile games like Dokobots and Seek n' Spell, made them an obvious choice to develop the audio framework to Moai, and we're thrilled to offer the UNTZ library now as part of the Moai Platform.

Please let us know what you think, and post your audio library wishlist items in the forums.
