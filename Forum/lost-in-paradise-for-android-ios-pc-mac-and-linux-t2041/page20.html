Re: Lost in Paradise - Released on Android! Launch Sale!!

by sshukul » Mon Nov 25, 2013 10:11 am

Thanks for the feedback scott and leaving the rating, much appreciated!

I was wondering about the text, went for something "authentic" looking for the era the story is set in but maybe it's too cursive, I'll see if I can find something a middle ground between readability and atmosphere.

The animations are a mixed bunch, some are straightforward curves, spins, fades and moves available in Moai, some involve low level pixel manipulation that I kind of innovated a bit, some are frames from videos my partner shot, but those are very resource heavy and I had to mess around with different techniques on integrating them, some are layered sprites etc.

Those were really going to be my "grand idea" for this game so I worked a lot on different techniques in the early days of development to get the effects just right. It was kinda tricky as I'm not an artist or animator but a programmer by trade so a lot of it was trial and error and innovating and building on techniques I saw elsewhere, but was fun to do something different than my usual stuff.
