Re: Don't Be Patchman: A Sneak-and-Grow Adventure

by naturally » Sun Mar 01, 2015 5:39 am

I did look into hiring a pro to do it. And maybe next time I will. But I am a bit of a perfectionist and I would have drove anyone I hired crazy, run up expenses, and not have been happy in the end. The trailer isn't perfect or even professional-level but it's got the spirit of what I wanted, plus it's all actual gameplay footage, I really love it and am super happy with it. It isn't for everyone, I was trying to make it into a "thinking-mans" trailer, part comedy, part philosophy.

It took almost 2 months to make this trailer, from the time I started to the end. One month was wasted learning how to because I came in delusional thinking I could whip it off quickly. At one point I even had to upgrade CPU/RAM/motherboard to properly capture 1080p at 60fps. In hindsight probably didn't need HD as 95% of people won't see it in HD. If you haven't seen it in HD I recommend you do, because it really looks like a game then.

Another thing about video editing. It's not just recording your game and pressing export. You do one take. The guy is too close up. You do another take. Good framing but tearing on video. Do that OVER AND OVER sometimes dozens of time for EACH tiny scene. Then try to edit it together. Hard drive keeps filling up. Then realize you need to fix some footage, get new footage, bla bla bla repeat repeat repeat. CREATING THIS TRAILER WAS AN INSANE AMOUNT OF WORK. And that's not including the programming, art, etc that went before it.

The most fun part tho was getting to do some coding for the title card sequences. As I've already mentioned a bunch of times, they were done in MOAI - everything in the trailer is 100% MOAI.
